Communication Channels

Real-Time (War Room)

  • Dedicated Slack/Teams channel
  • Video bridge for coordination
  • Technical discussion and decisions
  • Limit to active responders

Scheduled Updates

  • Email for formal updates
  • Regular cadence (hourly/daily)
  • Stakeholder-appropriate detail
  • Documented record

Incident Management

  • Ticketing system (ServiceNow, Jira)
  • Single source of truth
  • Audit trail
  • Compliance evidence

  • Assume compromise: Don't use potentially compromised systems
  • Out-of-band: Use separate channels (phone, personal devices)
  • Need-to-know: Limit detailed IOCs to responders
  • No speculation: Report facts, not assumptions

Detection Engineer's Role in Communication

üí° How Detection Engineers Support IR Communication

  • Alert context: Provide detection rule logic and why it fired
  • Historical data: Query for related activity and scope
  • IOC enrichment: Add threat intel context to indicators
  • Timeline generation: Build attack timeline from logs
  • Detection gaps: Identify what we missed and why
  • Improvement recommendations: New detections needed

Interview Preparation

Key principles:

  • Audience-appropriate: Technical for SOC, business impact for executives
  • Regular cadence: Set expectations for update frequency
  • Facts not speculation: Report what we know, not what we think
  • Single source of truth: Centralized incident ticket
  • Out-of-band if needed: Don't use compromised channels

Essential elements:

  • Executive summary (non-technical)
  • Timeline of events
  • Technical details (IOCs, affected systems)
  • Detection analysis (what worked, gaps)
  • Response analysis (effective actions, improvements)
  • Recommendations with owners and dates
  • Lessons learned
